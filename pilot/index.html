Engagement sequence

A controlled path from readiness review to signed finding.

Scope stays visible so the pilot answers a precise engineering question.

Scope and readiness

Agree the unit, question, evidence boundary, acceptance criteria and expected decision.

Reconstruct the basis

Ingest the studies, scenario credit, detector register and plant geometry with provenance.

Sweep and review

Run the credible physical space, review controlling cases and challenge preliminary gaps.

Sign and deliver

Issue the register, spatial findings, remediation priorities, method and limitations for approval.

A strong pilot fit

There is a real claim or decision to test.

  • 01A proposed layout needs independent challenge before issue
  • 02An existing mapping study no longer reflects the plant
  • 03An MOC, audit or incident has raised a coverage question
  • 04A team needs defensible priorities before spending capital

What the pilot is not

A certification shortcut or autonomous safety decision.

  • 01It does not certify the F&G system or replace a qualified engineer
  • 02It does not infer missing plant truth without declaring uncertainty
  • 03It does not require live OT data or control-system access
  • 04It does not convert weak evidence into optimistic credit
