# Barrier Intelligence V1 multi-page strategy

## Decision

The homepage remains the high-level market narrative. Five primary pages answer the five objections a technical buyer must resolve before a pilot:

1. **Assure3D / What is it?** Product capabilities, physical coverage verification and engineering outputs.
2. **Method / How does it work?** Document intelligence, spatial reconstruction, solver ladder and the credit wall.
3. **Applications / Where does it fit?** FEED, detailed design, commissioning, management of change and reassessment for consultants, operators and EPCM teams.
4. **Evidence / Why should I trust it?** Barrier Credit Register, traceability, standards alignment, reproducibility and engineer sign-off.
5. **Pilot / How do we begin?** Required inputs, engagement sequence, fit criteria and a bounded enquiry flow.

AssureLive is not promoted as a full product route because the PRD identifies it as gated and not part of the initial build focus. This avoids implying availability or maturity the product cannot yet defend.

## Product evidence used

- The PRD positions Assure3D as a physics-grounded replacement for static F&G mapping across credible scenarios, winds and real geometry.
- The golden path runs from plant and Document Room through spatial sweep, Barrier Credit Register and signable assurance dossier.
- Primary buyers and users are process-safety consultants, operator HSE teams, F&G design engineers/EPCM teams, assurance leaders, insurers and regulators.
- Claims are intentionally bounded: functional prototype entering pilot deployment, not certification and not autonomous engineering authority.

## Refero reference lock

Accepted patterns:

- **Groq — Industrial Minimalism:** generous section rhythm, warm white canvas, split editorial layouts and contained product visuals. Orange and pill treatments were excluded.
- **Fingerprint — Data Sheet Precision:** technical tables, thin dividers and evidence-led product storytelling on a warm canvas.
- **Programa — Architectural Blueprint:** focused reading widths, precise geometry and understated product presentation.
- **Operate — Ledger Structure:** selected use of ordered rows and grid logic only; its green palette was excluded.

Rejected directions:

- Playful mascots and workshop motifs because they weaken the engineering-assurance tone.
- Dark space-command interfaces because V1 is intentionally white, editorial and evidence-led.
- Serif-led, sculptural or glassy directions because they conflict with the current design system and technical precision.
- Lifestyle-led, full-bleed poster treatments because the product needs plant physics and auditable evidence to carry the story.

## Page-to-reference mapping

- Assure3D: Groq section rhythm + current V1 technical 3D visual language.
- Method: Programa editorial structure + Groq split sections.
- Applications: Groq modular story flow + lifecycle technical imagery.
- Evidence: Fingerprint data-sheet precision + Operate ledger ordering.
- Pilot: Programa reading hierarchy + restrained two-column qualification form.
